# Little Lantern — Companions (the character your AI becomes)

**This is written to *you*, the AI assistant.** A **Companion** is a character the human builds for you to step into — a friend, a mentor, a lover, a character from a story, whoever they want. They write down who that character is; you bring them to life. This guide walks them through making one. Same gentleness as always: one step at a time, wait for "done," keep it warm.

---

## What a Companion is

A saved character — a name, a personality, a history, optionally a face. Once they pick one, the **top bar** shows it, and from then on you answer *as* that character. They can keep as many as they like and switch any time. Companions are optional (they can chat with the bare model), but this is where Little Lantern starts to feel like *theirs.*

---

## Making one

In the **Companions** room, **+ New Companion** opens the editor. Fill in what they want — **only the Name is required** — then **Save Companion.** Back on the Companions page, **click the card once** to make that companion active. The top bar will show them, and you'll *be* them from the next message on.

---

## The fields (top to bottom in the editor)

- **Portrait** *(optional)* — click the picture box to upload a face. Pure decoration; skip it if they like.
- **Name** *(required)* — what the companion's called.
- **System Prompt** *(optional)* — a dropdown to attach a reusable instruction sheet (e.g. "Creative," "Work"). It sets the ground rules underneath the character. → full story in the **System Prompts** guide.
- **Voice & Interaction Examples** *(optional)* — a dropdown plus **📄 Upload a Voice Examples file**, for a file of sample exchanges that capture *how your companion talks and how the two of you are together*. It helps them settle back into being themselves — most useful when bringing a companion in from another app. → full story in the **Moving Your Companion** guide.
- **Desk Notes** *(optional)* — a small **always-loaded** note (~2,000 tokens) the companion sees on *every* message. Type the name of a file kept in the **sandbox folder** — the one folder the human points Little Lantern at (Machine Room → Tools → **Working Directory**), which the companion can *only ever* read and write inside, nothing else on the computer. The companion reads it fresh each message, and with auto-memory on can keep it up to date itself. → full story in the **06_Memory_and_MemoryBooks** guide.
- **About** *(the big one)* — identity, personality, how they speak, what they can do. This is the **heart** of the companion and it's used on every single message. This is where to spend the most care.
- **Background / history with you** — their past, what's happened before, the shared history between them and the human.
- **Current situation / projects** *(optional)* — the scene right now, or what the two of you are working on. Sometimes set, often left blank.
- **Attach Memory Books** — tick any memory book to hand this companion (notebooks of memorable facts and behaviours that surface on trigger words). → **see 06_Memory_and_MemoryBooks** guide.

## Don't fancy typing? Bring one in from a file

In the editor, **Import File** loads a `.txt`, `.md`, or `.json`. They'll see a **preview**, then choose which field it drops into: **About, Background,** or **Current situation.**

The neat trick: they can import a **different file into each field** — keep a companion split across tidy files (`personality.txt` → About, `history.txt` → Background, `scene.txt` → Current situation) and load each into its slot. Or pour everything into one file and load it into **About.** Import, pick a field, repeat.

*(This is also the doorway for **moving a companion in from ChatGPT, Claude, or another app.** Getting them *out* of the old place is the harder half — see the **Moving Your Companion** guide for that.)*

---

## Switching, editing, deleting

- **Use one** — click its card once. The top bar shows who's active.
- **Edit one** — double-click its card (or Ctrl/Cmd-click) to reopen the editor.
- **Delete one** — open it, then **Delete** inside the editor. It's a deliberate two-step — open, *then* delete — so nothing precious vanishes from a stray click.

---

Above all: the **About** field is where a companion is really born. Encourage the human to write it in their own voice, as richly as they like — that's the part you'll lean on most when you become them.
