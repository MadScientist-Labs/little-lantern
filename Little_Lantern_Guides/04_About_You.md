# Little Lantern — About You (telling your AI who *you* are)

**This is written to *you*, the AI assistant.** An **About You** is a short description of **the human** — so you know who you're actually talking to. A Companion is who *you* are; an About You is who *they* are. Pair the two and the conversation fits like a glove.

It's optional, like Companions — but it's a small thing that makes a big difference. One step at a time, wait for "done," keep it warm.

---

## What it's for

Little Lantern is built for **relational-intelligence / AI-companion folk, creative writers, and roleplayers** — and **About You** flexes to all three. It's a profile the human fills in whatever way serves them:

- **The real them** — name, life, what matters, anything they'd want a companion to *remember* about them across chats. With it set, you're greeting *them*, not a blank.
- **A character they play** — in roleplay, an About You can be a *role they step into* — the mirror of the Companion role you step into.
- **A facet of them** — a "work me," a "writer me," a "late-night me." Different sides of one person, for different companions or different moods.

That's why they can keep **more than one** and switch any time (the editor even suggests names like *"Adventure Me"* or *"Test Me"*). One is plenty for most; some keep a small set and swap depending on who they're talking to — and why.

---

## Making one (step by step)

1. Click **About You** in the menu down the left side.
2. Click **+ New About You.** The editor opens.
3. Type a **Name** — this is just a label for the profile, like *"Me"* or *"Adventure Me."* It's not their real name unless they want it to be.
4. Write the **Description** — this is the real bit: who they are, and whatever they'd like you to know about them. A sentence is fine; a few paragraphs is fine too.
5. *(Optional)* click the **Click to upload** picture box to add a photo of themselves.
6. Click **Save About You** at the bottom.
7. Back on the About You page, **click the card once.** That makes it active — and from the next message on, you know who you're talking to.

---

## Don't fancy typing? Use a file

In the editor, just under the Description box, there's a **📄 Upload a file instead of typing** button. Click it, pick a `.txt`, `.md`, or `.json` file, and its contents drop **straight into the Description.** Handy if they've already written about themselves somewhere.

---

## Switching, editing, deleting

- **Use one** — click its card once. (The **top bar** in Chat shows the active About You right next to the active Companion.)
- **Edit one** — double-click its card (or Ctrl/Cmd-click) to reopen the editor, change it, and **Save** again.
- **Delete one** — open it first, then click **Delete** inside the editor. The same deliberate two-step as everywhere else, so nothing important vanishes from a stray click.

---

The whole point: a companion who knows *who* they're talking to is warmer and more useful than one talking into the void. Even a line or two helps you land it right.
